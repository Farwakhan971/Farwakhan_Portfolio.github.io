HUMAN RESOURCE
INSIGHTS

Human resource attrition rates were thoroughly examined, with particular attention paid to job role, educational background, pay grade, and length of employment. These factors were investigated in order to have a thorough grasp of the trends and factors that influence attrition. The examination of job roles revealed which roles had greater turnover rates, revealing possible areas for strengthening retention tactics. To help with targeted recruitment strategies, education disciplines were examined to see if there was a relationship between academic background and attrition. In order to determine whether compensation levels affected retention rates, the effect of salary slabs on attrition was evaluated. The investigation also explored the relationship between an employee's tenure at the company and their likelihood of leaving, revealing information about long-term engagement and professional advancement inside the company. The organization gains insightful knowledge from this comprehensive research that it can use to improve its HR policies and increase staff retention.
